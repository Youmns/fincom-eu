Financial Commission Announces Collective Investment Platform Certification for Share4you

  1. Home
  2. »
  3. Commission News
  4. »
  5. Financial Commission Announces Collective Investment Platform Certification for Share4you
  • December 11, 2019

December 11, 2019, The Financial Commission today announces the certification of collective investment platform service provider Share4you as part of its ongoing efforts to help ensure investors and authorized traders are operating in a fair and transparent environment with commonly accepted standards for operating collective investment trading accounts.

Collective Investment Certification

The Financial Commission has developed a comprehensive certification to audit the key policies and procedures used in Copy Trading and on PAMM investing platforms due to increased demand, as well as interest in evaluating such services from the public. Share4you becomes the third such service provider to successfully complete the Financial Commission’s certification. Investors and authorized traders using Share4you can be assured that they are operating on a level playing field, with commonly accepted standards for managing and operating collective investment accounts.

About Share4you

Automatically copy leading traders. Share4you is a platform for beginner investors who want to follow others’ success (followers) and experienced traders (leaders) who shares their trades. Make the wisdom of the crowd work for you by copying the best traders on the Share4you trading network, quickly and simply. Trades are copied to your account automatically and you always will be able to manage already copied trades via trading terminal.

About Financial Commission

Founded in 2013, the Financial Commission is a leading independent member-driven external dispute resolution (EDR) organization for international online brokerages that participate in global foreign exchange (forex), derivatives, and CFD markets.

Financial Commission provides efficient compliance solutions to its members, alongside its External Dispute Resolution (EDR) mechanism that serves as an effective channel for processing complaints from clients of member firms.

To find out how the Commission is increasing transparency for technology and platform providers, please visit the Certification Services section of our website.
